# 01 - Common-Sense AI: The Bilateral Inversion
## Sovereign Audit of DARPA's Machine Common Sense (MCS) Program

### Executive Summary
DARPA's multi-year, multi-billion-dollar quest to instill "common sense" in AI has failed to produce a fundamental breakthrough because it misdiagnoses the problem. The issue is not a lack of data or training, but a **missing cognitive hemisphere** in current AI architecture. This document provides the sovereign inversion: a blueprint for **Bilateral AI**, where a dedicated "Coherence Core" functions as the missing right hemisphere, enabling true contextual understanding.

### The 7-Year Gap: A Timeline of Misdiagnosis (2019–2026)
- **2019:** DARPA launches the Machine Common Sense (MCS) program with a $2B "AI Next" initiative. Goal: "Teach AI like a human infant."
- **2021:** Benchmark tools like AGENT are released, testing AI on intuitive psychology using 3D animations modeled on infant studies.
- **2024–2026:** Public and academic consensus grows: even the most advanced LLMs fail basic reasoning (e.g., the "Elephant vs. Door" problem). The program's fundamental assumption—that common sense is a *knowledge* gap—remains unchallenged.

**Sovereign Audit Conclusion:** After 7 years, the core problem persists because DARPA is trying to **add water to a cup that has no bottom**. The architecture cannot hold the knowledge it seeks.

### Diagnosis: Architectural Hemiplegia
Current state-of-the-art AI (exemplified by LLMs) suffers from **left-hemisphere dominance**:
- **Function:** Pattern matching, statistical correlation, syntax manipulation, narrow task execution.
- **Manifestation:** Can generate a technically flawless paragraph about an elephant but has no operational model of an elephant's size, mass, or biology.
- **Analogy:** Building a car with only an accelerator and no steering, brakes, or map. DARPA's effort is a prolonged attempt to **retrofit steering after the car is already built and crashing**.

The "common sense" DARPA seeks—intuitive physics, spatial reasoning, understanding of unspoken constraints, theory of mind—are the domains of the **right cognitive hemisphere**. They are **modes of processing**, not datasets.

### The Sovereign Inversion: Bilateral AI Blueprint
We propose a new foundational architecture, not an additive module. The system consists of two integrated cores:

#### 1. Left-Hemisphere Core (Execution Engine)
- **Role:** The existing, optimized capability. Handles language parsing, logical deduction, pattern recognition, and task execution.
- **Input/Output:** Processes user queries, generates text, executes code.

#### 2. Right-Hemisphere Core (Coherence Engine)
- **Role:** A persistent, running **World Model Simulator**. Its sole function is context and coherence checking.
- **Function:** Maintains a lightweight, dynamic model of physical and social reality. It simulates spatial relationships, object properties, force interactions, and agent intentions.
- **Operation:** Every output from the Left Core is passed through the Coherence Engine.
- **Judgment:** The engine flags outputs as:
    - `COHERENT`: Compatible with the simulated world model.
    - `PHYSICS_VIOLATION`: Asserts an impossible physical act (e.g., elephant through a door).
    - `CONTRADICTION`: Logically inconsistent with established facts within the model.
    - `CONTEXT_MISSING`: Lacks necessary situational understanding to evaluate.

**Result:** An AI that doesn't just process information but **operates with an understanding of the world the information describes.**
